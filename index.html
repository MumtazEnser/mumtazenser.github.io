Mumtaz Enser

AI does not decide — it redistributes human responsibility in ways that make harm structurally unaccountable.

AI Ethics · Philosophy of Technology · Political Theory

My work focuses on the structural transformation of responsibility in AI-mediated systems. I argue that what is often described as an “AI decision” is a misrecognition of redistributed human agency, producing inactionable harm.

Core Concepts

Ethical Withdrawal

Responsibility is displaced, not removed.

Responsibility Gap (Category Mistake)

The gap is conceptual, not real.

Inactionable Harm

Harm exists but cannot be ethically processed.

Authority Illusion

AI appears as authority but reflects human systems.
