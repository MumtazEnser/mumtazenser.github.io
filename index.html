Research Statement

My work develops a relational theory of responsibility for complex institutional and socio-technical systems. Drawing on Wittgenstein, Kant, and Arendt, I argue that the so-called "responsibility gap" in AI ethics is not a genuine absence of responsibility but a conceptual and ontological mistake — arising from an individualistic model of agency that fails to account for distributed, practice-based structures of action.

My current research examines the architecture of moral and institutional responsibility across three intersecting domains: the philosophy of artificial intelligence, political philosophy of technology, and the history of analytic philosophy. I am particularly concerned with how decision authority is structured and concealed within AI governance systems, and with the normative implications of what I call ethical withdrawal — the displacement of accountability onto procedures, systems, or institutions that renders responsibility anonymous.
